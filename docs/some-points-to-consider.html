4 Some points to consider

4.1 Downscaling

It might be interesting to downscale the analysis to Statistics Canada’s census subdivision scale. An example of such an approach was used by (adegun2021?) in Manitoba. Before I can do that, however, I would need to rework the health regions provided by the covidtimelinecanada project, since they are not the same as the health regions from Statistics Canada. There is a short description of the modifications made to the shapefiles in the covidtimelinecanada project, but essentially it boils down to Saskatchewan and British-Columbia having different covid reporting regions than the Statistics Canada health regions. Furthermore, the outline of health regions from the covid data is too simplified, I’m losing a lot of data along the coast. This means that I must either modify the polygons, or use an analysis to identify the closest polygons to each placenames rather than an intersect approach.
