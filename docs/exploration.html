3.1.1 Cumulative vulnerabilities

Cumulative vulnerability is the sum of the 15 social vulnerabilities currently considered normalized between 0 and 1, used as a proxy of the relative intensity of each social vulnerability measurement.

Figure 3.1: Cumulative vulnerabilities of Canadian communities, measured as a function of the relative intensity of 15 social vunlrability measurements.

3.1.2 Cumulative COVID-19 cases

Figure 3.2: Cumulative cases in Canadian communities. Cases were measured at the scale of health regions and extracted for each community based on which health region it is a part of. Cumulative cases are thus not representative of cumulative cases within a specific community, but rather of the health region to which they belong
